Video Simulations

Natural disasters can be difficult for young people to understand if they have never experience one.

So simulations show what it actually looks and feels like to be in a disaster - how quickly events unfold, how people move, where danger zones are, and how to escape safely.

These videos were created to simulate natural disasters based on real-world case scenarios. To avoid depicting real tragedies and to maintain ethical standards, an AI generator (LTX Studio) was used to produce fictional representations.

This fictional city was fully designed and animated in Blender.

The concept behind it is to help young audiences understand that cities can exist in many different environments — from mountainsides to coastlines — and each unique location can be affected by different types of natural disasters. Many disasters might seem unfamiliar or unlikely in your own city, but they can be very real and devastating elsewhere.

To reflect this, I created a city positioned in a high-risk area that could realistically be affected by multiple natural hazards. The animation incorporates real-world case scenarios to highlight the dangers and raise awareness about how natural disasters can impact various environments.
